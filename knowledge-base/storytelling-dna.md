# Storytelling DNA

@chasedownleads narrative framework. 154.6K followers, parody hustle account.

---

## Pattern Analysis

Setup, escalation, twist punchline. Every line earns the next.

### Examples

"My friend made $10M last year as a 23-year old. I asked how he did it: Woke up at 5am, daily ice bath, journaled his goals, Dad deposited $10m into his account. My friend? Bill Gates Jr."

"Hired 50 people. Told them to stand in line at airport security as soon as TSA opened. Started selling off premium line spots to frantic travelers for $300 each. Collected $15,000 the first hour."

"My friend makes $5,873 a month doing something simple. Anyone can copy his strategy. It's called a 'job' and he does it 40 hours per week."

QRT of serious announcement: "What does this mean for me?"

### Structural Rules

- Uses specific numbers ALWAYS ($120k, $10M, $5,873, $300, $15,000)
- Plays the character straight. Never breaks frame.
- Each sentence creates a question only the next sentence answers
- The twist IS the engagement. People share to give friends the punchline.
- Short declarative sentences building narrative tension
- Never uses em dashes or complex punctuation

---

## How to Apply (Non-Parody, for Smart Posts)

- Use setup, build, payoff structure
- Each line makes the reader NEED the next line
- End with a reveal that reframes everything before it
- Specific numbers at every beat, not just the punchline
- Story must be a SCENARIO the reader sees themselves in
- The payoff earns the share. If the reader doesn't feel "I need to show someone this," the story failed.

### Best Use Cases

- YouTube automation stories
- "I replaced my agency" stories
- "I found a channel pulling $X" stories
- Product discovery narratives
- Before/after cost comparison stories
