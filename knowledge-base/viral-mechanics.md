# Viral Mechanics

8 empirically-proven viral mechanisms from analysis of 1,176 Twitter/X posts (775 unique accounts, 78 posts with 1M+ views).

---

## M1: Specific Numbers as Credibility Proxies

Posts with specific numbers average 3.2x more views than those without.

"512,000 lines at 4AM" = 3.7M views.
"A lot of code" = 45K views.
Same story, 80x reach difference.

Specific numbers signal insider knowledge. They make the reader believe the poster has access to real data, not opinion.

---

## M2: Emotional Setup Before Reveal

Every 3M+ post sets up emotion BEFORE delivering information.

"forever. read that again."
"What happened next is one of the most insane stories in tech history."

The emotional frame tells the reader HOW to feel about the information before they receive it. Without the frame, the same information underperforms by 5-10x.

---

## M3: Proxy Audience Identification

Name the specific affected group. 4x engagement increase.

"grad students and junior hires are cooked" beats "many professionals may be affected."

When readers see their group named, they tag friends, quote-tweet to their community, and feel personally addressed. Generic audience references get scrolled past.

---

## M4: Impossible Instruction as Product Proof

"Claude, jiggle my cursor so Teams stays green while I nap."

Doesn't SAY it's powerful. DEMONSTRATES a use case so specific only a real user would think of it.

The specificity of the instruction IS the proof. Nobody invents "jiggle my cursor so Teams stays green" unless they've actually wanted that. The absurdity makes it shareable. The specificity makes it believable.

---

## M5: Institutional Failure + Named Individual

"Anthropic leaked source code" = 80K views.
"Researcher Chaofan Shou spotted it in 23 minutes" = 3.7M views.

Named individuals convert abstract institutional stories into shareable human narratives. A person finding a flaw is a story. A company having a flaw is a press release.

---

## M6: Competitive Zero-Sum Frame

Framing one company's gain as another's loss outperforms parallel framing by 3-5x.

"OpenAI just killed Google's moat" beats "OpenAI launches new search feature."

Conflict generates views. Coexistence doesn't. Readers share conflict because it invites a side. Neutral announcements invite nothing.

---

## M7: Official Source Multiplier

Official accounts get 27x more reach than third-party accounts posting the same information.

Third-party posts need credibility hooks to compensate:
- Insider source reference
- Specific data points
- Named individual
- Personal experience with the product

Without at least one credibility hook, third-party posts about company news plateau at 50-80K regardless of content quality.

---

## M8: Consequence Closing

Every post above 3M views ends with CONSEQUENCE, not capability.

"forever. read that again." not "it can do X."
"Anthropic didn't buy them.. they replaced them.." not "Anthropic ships computer use."

Consequence closers tell the reader what to FEEL. Capability closers tell the reader what to KNOW. Feelings get shared. Knowledge gets bookmarked and forgotten.
