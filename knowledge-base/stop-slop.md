# Stop Slop

Anti-AI writing checklist from Hardik Pandya's stop-slop framework, extended with callum-bot specific checks.

---

## Core Rules

1. **Cut filler phrases.** Remove throat-clearing openers, emphasis crutches, all adverbs.
2. **Break formulaic structures.** No binary contrasts, negative listings, dramatic fragmentation, rhetorical setups, false agency.
3. **Use active voice.** Every sentence needs a human subject doing something.
4. **Be specific.** No vague declaratives. Name the specific thing.
5. **Put the reader in the room.** "You" beats "People." Specifics beat abstractions.
6. **Vary rhythm.** Mix sentence lengths. Two items beat three. No em dashes.
7. **Trust readers.** State facts directly. Skip softening and justification.
8. **Cut quotables.** If it sounds like a pull-quote, rewrite it.

---

## Quick Checks

- Any adverbs? Kill them.
- Any passive voice? Find the actor.
- Inanimate thing doing human verb? Name the person.
- "here's what/this/that"? Cut to point.
- "not X, it's Y"? State Y directly.
- Three sentences match length? Break one.
- Paragraph ends with punchy one-liner? Vary it.
- Em-dash? Remove it.
- Vague declarative? Name the specific implication.

---

## Scoring

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Directness | Statements or announcements? |
| Rhythm | Varied or metronomic? |
| Trust | Respects reader intelligence? |
| Authenticity | Sounds human? Sounds like THIS influencer? |
| Density | Anything cuttable? |

Each dimension scored 1-10. Total out of 50.

Below 35/50: revise.
Below 40/50: revise for callum-bot standard.

---

## Additional Anti-AI Checks (callum-bot specific)

- **"Would a real human text this to their friend?"** If no, rewrite.
- Any sentence that sounds like: LinkedIn post, product page, press release, corporate blog? Rewrite.
- "The cost structure in X is collapsing" becomes "bro this costs $2 now"
- "shifted ENTIRELY" becomes letting numbers speak or using a casual reaction
- Summarizing instead of showing? Show the spectacle.
- Perfect grammar when influencer's voice is casual? Add human texture.
- Abstract categories ("sports media") become specific examples ("football highlights channels")
- Overly parallel sentence structures? Break the pattern.
