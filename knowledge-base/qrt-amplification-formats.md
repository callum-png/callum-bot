# QRT Amplification Formats

Reference examples for quote-retweeting brand announcements. These formats frame the product post so the reader knows HOW to feel about it. The QRT does the selling — the quoted tweet is just proof underneath.

Source: @shiri_shh (1.1M views), @cgtwts (859K views), @EXM7777 (423K views) — all QRTing Base44 announcements.

---

## Format A: "People who X... now watching Y" (Schadenfreude)

**Pattern:** One sentence. Old buyer/old way is the loser. New thing makes them look dumb.

**Example:**
```
People who spent $599 on a Mac Mini to run OpenClaw… now watching Base44 give you the same thing + 130 Skills for just $20/month
```

**Why it works:**
- Reader either laughs at the loser or realizes THEY are the loser
- One sentence = maximum density, zero filler
- Specific dollar contrast ($599 vs $20/month) does all the persuading
- "now watching" = they can't do anything about it. The damage is done.

**Higgsfield adaptation:**
```
People who paid $48K to a UGC agency... now watching someone make 19 ad variations in 90 minutes on a $15 app
```
```
People who spent $60K on a single VFX shot... now watching a kid do the same thing from a text prompt for $15/month
```

## Format B: "RIP to X" + Feature Bullet Dump

**Pattern:** Emotional death sentence opener → > bullet list of capabilities → casual one-line summary

**Example:**
```
RIP to everyone who just bought a $600 Mac Mini

Base44 just dropped Superagent.
> persistent memory
> scheduled jobs
> event triggers
> browser sessions

It's basically an agent that runs your workflows on autopilot and is very simple to use.
```

**Why it works:**
- "RIP" is instant emotional hook — someone died (figuratively)
- Bullet list is PROOF, not specs. Each line is a capability.
- Casual summary at the end ("it's basically...") makes it accessible
- The reader scrolls down to the quoted tweet to verify the claim

**Higgsfield adaptation:**
```
RIP to every UGC agency charging $48K

Seedance 2.0 just dropped on Higgs.
> text to video
> 12 assets per generation
> character consistency across shots
> native audio sync

its basically a full production studio for $15/month and it works
```

## Format C: "Year vs Year" Before/After Comparison

**Pattern:** "[thing] in [old year]:" → pain bullets / "[thing] in [new year]:" → easy bullets. Last line is the kicker.

**Example:**
```
startups in 2024:
> raise $500K
> hire a design agency
> wait 2 months for a website
> launch with a site that looks like every other YC company

startups in 2026:
> find the YC company site they wish they had
> paste it into Base44
> launch with a better version in the same afternoon
```

**Why it works:**
- The CONTRAST does all the persuading
- Each bullet in the "old" section is a pain point the reader recognizes
- Each bullet in the "new" section is absurdly easy by comparison
- Last line is always the kicker that makes it feel unfair
- Relatable scenario (everyone knows a startup that did this)

**Higgsfield adaptation:**
```
video ads in 2024:
> hire a UGC agency for $48K
> wait 3 weeks for 3 options
> pick the least bad one
> pray it converts

video ads in 2026:
> upload one product photo to Higgs
> type what u want
> get 19 variations in 90 minutes
> test all of them for $15
```

## Rules for ALL Amplification QRTs

1. **SHORT.** None of these are over 5 lines of real content. Shorter = more views.
2. **Specific dollar amounts ALWAYS.** $599, $600, $500K, $20/month — never vague.
3. **Product name appears ONCE.** Naturally, not forced.
4. **QRT standalone test MUST pass.** Reader should get the point without reading/watching the quoted tweet.
5. **Someone is LOSING.** The old way, the old buyer, the old agency. There's always a loser in amplification QRTs.
6. **The QRT FRAMES the announcement.** It tells the reader how to feel: shocked, amused, or left behind.
7. **No storytelling.** These are reactions, not narratives. Save stories for standalone posts.
8. **Use > bullet format** for feature dumps or before/after lists (Twitter renders these cleanly).
