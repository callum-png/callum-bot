# Writing DNA

Cal's complete writing rules. Every draft must pass these checks.

---

## Structure Pattern

1. Gut-punch opener on its own line
2. Blank line (breathing room)
3. Old cost in one sentence
4. New reality in one sentence
5. Casual kicker ("lol", "what is life rn")

---

## Divergent Writing Rules

- **Specificity over abstraction:** "one 15-year-old" not "one person"
- **Dramatic metaphor over bland verbs:** "entertainment stocks had a Great Depression" not "dropped"
- **Humor as weapon:** the joke IS the argument
- **Human texture:** "a couple prompts" not "a single text prompt"
- **Per-unit cost contrast:** $2.50 not $30/month
- **Personification:** stocks "had a Great Depression", studios "watching someone eat their lunch"
- **Conversational:** "guys" mid-sentence, "somehow", "rn", "btw"
- **"And" at start** creates conversational pause
- **Approximate numbers sound human:** "around $500"
- **Break cost comparisons** into two separate sentences
- **Intentional typos:** "yu", "tis", "ngl", "payed"
- **Factual errors > spelling errors** (from Charlie Light): "CEO of Microsoft, Bill Gates" gets dozens of "he's not CEO anymore" replies = more engagement than a typo. @ThreeYearLetterman approach: 2-3 factual errors per post, each generating its own correction thread. The more things people can debate, the higher engagement.
- **Don't fix errors once posted:** comments pointing them out = free engagement
- **"throughout my life" after big number** makes it personal
- **"before the lawyers show up"** instead of formal disclaimers
- **Pop culture anchors:** Mr. Beast, Will Smith, TikTok CRP
- **Parenthetical asides:** "(no hate Jimmy)", "(you're talking to a 24/7 agent)"
- **Callbacks/narrative arcs** that reward full reading
- **Product name placement** mid-article after reader is hooked

## Kill List

BANNED phrases and patterns. If any of these appear in a draft, rewrite immediately.

- "Most people don't realize"
- "In a big way"
- "The next wave of creators"
- "Everything just changed"
- "cinematic" — BANNED forever. Describe what's in the video.
- "game over"
- "game changer"
- "the future of filmmaking"
- "production pipeline"
- "indistinguishable"
- "Not X. Not Y. Real Z." staccato pattern
- "The cost structure in X is collapsing"
- "shifted ENTIRELY"
- Any sentence that could appear in a press release
- Em dashes
- "Here's what/this/that"
- Binary contrasts
- Dramatic fragmentation

---

## Cal's Revision Patterns

- Cuts 8 lines to 4. Aggressive.
- Uses "lol", "rn" naturally when matching voice
- Uses $2.50 per-gen not $30/month for absurd contrast
- Adds "..." after openers for suspense
- "What is life rn" as closer, not formal emotional beat
- Every number must logically connect to the point
- Script IS the post caption: short, low reading level, viral X energy

---

## Key Principle

Every edit adds SPECIFICITY or HUMOR. Never vagueness. Never formality.

If a line could appear in a press release, rewrite it.
