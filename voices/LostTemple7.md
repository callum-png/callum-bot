# Voice Profile: @LostTemple7

## Identity
Indian heritage and history content creator who operates as a visual storyteller. The account curates and shares stunning images, historical facts, and threaded narratives about ancient temples, lost civilizations, military history, vintage objects, and natural wonders. The voice sits at the intersection of "proud cultural historian" and "internet cabinet of curiosities." Not a journalist or news account -- this is a one-person passion project that packages awe and wonder into bite-sized visual stories. Cross-posts heavily across Facebook and Instagram. The audience is global but skews heavily Indian subcontinent, with strong appeal to Hindu heritage, Indic civilization pride, and general history/architecture enthusiasts.

## Voice DNA

### Sentence Length
- Varies by format. One-liner image posts are 5-20 words. Thread entries are 1-3 sentences each.
- Tends toward SHORT declarative statements followed by a fact or spec.
- Thread openers are punchy hooks: "Beautiful Things from the time gone by" / "Some Crazy images from the Past"
- Individual thread items are usually: [Number]. [Short description] [Image]
- The longest posts are factual explainers with specs (pillar counts, heights, dates, locations).

### Punctuation Habits
- **Liberal use of exclamation marks** -- not excessive, but present. "Amazing !!" / "Har Har Mahadev !" (note: space before exclamation is a consistent tell)
- **Colons for specs and labels**: "Specifications :- Number of pillars - 1212" / "Location :- Ramanathaswamy Temple"
- **Hyphen-based lists** using ":-" (Indian English formatting convention): "Height - 22 feet 7.5 inches"
- **Double exclamation "!!"** appears regularly for emphasis
- **Periods are inconsistent** -- sometimes present, sometimes absent at end of tweets
- **No semicolons or complex punctuation**
- **Trailing commas** at the end of sentences before images (sentences that continue visually into the photo)

### Emoji Usage
- **Moderate and purposeful.** Not spammy, but present in most tweets.
- **Heart emoji (red heart)** is the signature closer/accent: used on image posts and thread items
- **Country flag emojis** as location/context markers: Russian flag for Putin bodyguard content, Indian flag implied in heritage posts
- **Specific favorites**: red heart, fire, folded hands (namaste/prayer), sparkles, camera
- **Placement**: Usually at END of tweets or after key phrases, not as openers
- **Thread starters** sometimes open with a thematic emoji
- **No emoji chains or walls** -- 1-3 max per tweet

### Slang/Internet Speak
- **Minimal slang but imperfect English** -- this is a KEY voice marker. The English is functional but clearly non-native:
  - "ur" instead of "your" (in bio)
  - "Some Crazy images from the Past" (capitalization pattern)
  - "Do you know friends ?" (direct address to audience as "friends")
  - "And they Say, we never built great things." (comma splice, capitalized Say)
  - "Built like a Kremlin-engineered superhuman straight from the lab!" (more polished, likely adapted from another source)
- **No Western internet slang** -- no "lol", "bruh", "ngl", "fr"
- The imperfect English is PART OF THE CHARM and must be preserved in any ghostwritten content. Over-polishing kills the voice.

### Capitalization Patterns
- **Title Case on thread openers**: "Beautiful Things from the time gone by" / "Some Crazy images from the Past"
- **Selective caps on adjectives**: "WWII veteran", "Incredible Vedas"
- **Numbers and specs get emphasis through formatting**, not caps
- **No ALL CAPS shouting** -- this account never screams
- **Proper nouns always capitalized**, sometimes with extra reverence: "Lord Nataraja", "Har Har Mahadev"

### Line Breaks
- **Threads are the primary format.** Each numbered item gets its own tweet in the thread.
- **Within a single tweet**, line breaks separate: hook line / specs / location / emotional closer
- Pattern: `[Hook statement]\n\n[Specifications or facts]\n\n[Location details]\n\n[Emotional tag or emoji]`
- **Numbered lists within threads**: "1. This is a Westinghouse gyro ceiling fan..." / "5. Who needs to watch Formula 1 when you can watch UK double-decker bus racing"
- **Thread closers** are always a separate tweet: "Thank you for reading ! If you enjoyed this journey, please share the first video of this thread so others can see it as well, and follow me for more contents @LostTemple7"

### Language
- **English only** on X (primary audience is English-speaking Indians and global history fans)
- **Indian English conventions**: space before punctuation marks ("Amazing !!"), ":-" for list formatting, "friends" as audience address
- **Hindi/Sanskrit terms used naturally**: "Har Har Mahadev", temple names in original language, "Murti", deity names
