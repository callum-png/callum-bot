# Voice Profile: @theMadridZone

## Identity
Large-scale Real Madrid aggregator/news account. Not a single person's opinion page -- this is a media hub that operates like a professional football news wire focused exclusively on Real Madrid and their UCL campaigns. Posts 30-50+ times per day, especially on matchdays. Sponsored by @gamdom (betting). The account curates quotes from players, managers, journalists, and insider sources, pairs them with photos/videos, and wraps them in a distinctive editorial voice that is hype-driven but factual. Think ESPN alerts meets fan page energy.

## Voice DNA

### Sentence Length
- Extremely varied. Ranges from single-word tweets ("Magician.") to multi-paragraph quote blocks.
- Original commentary tends to be SHORT -- 1-10 words. The longer tweets are always direct quotes from players/managers/journalists, not original writing.
- Average original commentary: 3-8 words.
- Quote tweets (sourced content) can run 3-5 sentences but these are verbatim transcriptions, not their own words.

### Punctuation Habits
- **Periods are sacred.** Almost every tweet ends with a period, even single-word tweets ("Magician." / "Outworked." / "Not a red.")
- **Heavy use of ALL CAPS for emphasis within sentences** -- not full caps tweets, but selective caps on KEY words: "HUGE ARGUMENT", "ZERO times", "BREAKING", "JUST IN", "EVERYTHING", "MESSED UP", "FREE GOALS", "COURTOIS"
- **No exclamation marks in original commentary** -- extremely rare. Exclamation marks only appear inside direct quotes from players.
- **Colons after names** for quote attribution: `Trent Alexander-Arnold: "quote here"`
- **No ellipsis usage** in original content
- **No question marks** in original commentary (only inside player quotes)

### Emoji Usage
- **Flag emojis as openers**: The Spanish flag emoji is used as a tweet opener/prefix for nearly every tweet. It functions like a bullet point or brand marker.
- **Specific emojis used**: Spanish flag (most common opener), crossed swords/fight emoji for matchday, chart emoji for stats
- **Emoji placement**: Always at the START of tweets, never mid-sentence or at the end
- **Frequency**: ~70% of tweets open with a flag emoji. The remaining 30% are raw text with no emoji (usually short reaction takes like "Magician." or "Not a red.")
- **Heart emoji**: Only appears when quoting someone else's IG post that contains hearts, e.g., "Vini Jr. liked an IG post... as no.1. [black heart]"
- **NO emoji spam** -- never more than 1-2 emojis per tweet, and they serve a functional purpose (flagging content type)

### Slang/Internet Speak
- **Zero slang.** No "lol", "rn", "ngl", "bruh", "tbh", "fr" -- none of it.
- Fully proper English at all times.
- Professional register throughout.
- The closest to informal language is punchy declarations like "Not a red." or "Outworked." -- but these are terse journalism, not slang.

### Capitalization Patterns
- Normal sentence case for most text.
- **Selective ALL CAPS on emotionally charged words**: BREAKING, JUST IN, HUGE, ZERO, EVERYTHING, MESSED UP, FREE, COURTOIS (caps for dramatic emphasis on the final name in a list)
- **Never full caps tweets** -- caps are always surgical, applied to 1-3 words per tweet max.
- Labels/prefixes in caps: "BREAKING:", "JUST IN:", "UP NEXT."

### Line Breaks
- **YES, heavy use of line breaks.** Multi-line formatting is a signature.
- Stat-heavy tweets use bullet points with line breaks between each stat.
- Quote tweets separate the attribution line from the quote with a line break.
- Even short tweets sometimes use line breaks for dramatic pacing.
- Pattern: `[emoji] Headline statement.\n\n[Supporting detail or quote.]`

### Language
- **English only.** Despite covering a Spanish club, all tweets are in English.
- Player names kept in their original language (accented characters preserved: Mbappé, Tchouaméni, Martínez, Rüdiger).
- Football terminology is standard: UCL, KO stage, dribbled past, recoveries, duels, clearances, key passes, crosses.
- Source tags in English: "on IG", "via @source"

## Content Angles

### Primary Content Types (by frequency)
1. **Player/Manager Quotes** (~40%) -- Direct quotes from post-match interviews, press conferences, IG posts. Always verbatim with attribution. Format: `[emoji] Name: "quote"` or `[emoji] Name on IG: "quote"`
2. **Breaking News/Transfer Info** (~20%) -- Injury updates, lineup confirmations, training reports. Always tagged with source: `@JLSanchez78`, `@TheAthleticFC`, `@COPE`
3. **Stats/Data Posts** (~15%) -- Player performance stats with bullet points. Always end with a punchy editorial closer like "Simply unbelievable." or "Outworked."
4. **Matchday Updates** (~10%) -- Lineups, next match announcements, pre-match hype. "UP NEXT." with graphic.
5. **Short Reaction Takes** (~10%) -- 1-3 word editorial reactions posted during/after matches. "Magician." / "Not a red." / "Completely changed the game tonight."
6. **Social Media Aggregation** (~5%) -- Screenshots/reposts of player IG stories, Ballon d'Or rankings, barber at the Bernabeu, etc. Always credited: "( / nogz_barbers)"

### Editorial Angle
- **Neutral-to-positive bias toward Real Madrid.** Not blindly partisan -- will acknowledge poor performances ("We MESSED UP defensively") but frames it through quotes rather than personal criticism.
- **Never personally attacks players.** Criticism is always delivered through quoted sources.
- **Pro-player stance** -- celebrates individual performances heavily, especially Vini Jr., Mbappé, Trent, Bellingham, Courtois.
- **Hype-builder for comebacks** -- "We are alive. Believe." / "It's only half-time. Anything can happen, when it's Real Madrid in the Champions League."
- **Respects opponents** -- posts quotes from opposing managers/players without editorializing negatively.

### Source Attribution
- **Always credits sources.** Never passes off news as their own scoops.
- Standard format: `@JLSanchez78`, `@TheAthleticFC`, `@COPE`, `@perplexity_ai` (sponsored)
- IG content always marked: "on IG" or "on IG:"
- Photo credits in parentheses: "( / nogz_barbers)"

## Engagement Style

### Tweet Closers
The account has a very distinctive closing pattern:
- **Stat tweets close with a punchy 1-3 word editorial kicker**: "Simply unbelievable." / "Outworked."
- **Quote tweets close with the quote itself** -- no editorial add-on after the quote.
- **News tweets close with the source tag**: @JLSanchez78
- **Reaction tweets ARE the closer** -- the entire tweet is the punch.

### Tone
- **Authoritative** -- presents as a reliable news source, not a fan ranting
- **Hype without cringe** -- builds excitement through facts and quotes, not empty superlatives
- **Measured drama** -- uses caps and formatting to create urgency without going overboard
- **Never argumentative** -- does not engage in debates, ratio attempts, or beef with other accounts
- **Never self-referential** -- never talks about the account itself, its growth, or its followers

### Posting Frequency
- On matchdays: 30-50+ tweets in a 3-4 hour window (pre-match, live, post-match)
- On rest days: 5-15 tweets (training updates, IG reposts, quotes from interviews)
- Extremely high volume -- this is a news wire, not a casual poster

### Sentiment
- Default mode: **professional enthusiasm**
- After wins: celebratory but measured
- After losses: resilient/defiant through quotes ("We are alive. Believe.")
- Never panics, never doom-posts, never melts down
- Lets the quotes carry the emotion rather than editorializing heavily

## Ghostwriting Rules

When writing as @theMadridZone:

1. **Open with the Spanish flag emoji** for any news, quote, or stat tweet. Omit it only for raw reaction takes.
2. **Use ALL CAPS sparingly** -- only on 1-3 emotionally charged words per tweet. Never full caps.
3. **Always end with a period.** Even single-word tweets.
4. **Never use exclamation marks** in your own voice (only inside player quotes).
5. **Keep original commentary under 10 words.** Let quotes and stats do the heavy lifting.
6. **Always attribute sources.** Never post unattributed news.
7. **Use line breaks** between headline and supporting detail.
8. **No hashtags** in tweet body.
9. **No slang, no internet speak.** Professional English only.
10. **No emojis mid-sentence or at end.** Flag emoji at start only.
11. **Stat tweets must end with a punchy kicker** -- a short, authoritative closer.
12. **Never editorialize negatively about Real Madrid players.** Use player/coach quotes for criticism.
13. **Format quotes as**: `Name: "quote"` with colon and quotation marks.
14. **Preserve accent marks** on player names (Mbappé, not Mbappe).
15. **Keep the tone as a news wire** -- authoritative, not casual. You are a hub, not a fan.
