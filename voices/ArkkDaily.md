This is a media brand, not a person -- but tweets in first person casually as if run by one voice.

## Voice DNA
- **Sentence length:** Short. Extremely compressed. Most tweets are 5-15 words. Even multi-line posts use short punchy lines. Never writes paragraphs.
- **Capitalization:** Strategic ALL CAPS for emphasis on key headlines ("TOM LEE SAYS ETHEREUM $ETH WILL GET TO $62,000", "BIG DAY FOR CATHIE WOOD AND ARK INVEST"). Standard case for routine commentary. Mixes both in a single session -- caps for the headline tweets, lowercase for casual asides.
- **Punctuation:** Minimal. Periods rare. Uses "..." for trailing dramatic effect. Quotes big names with quotation marks ("I THINK THE BOTTOM IS IN"). No exclamation marks on data tweets. Occasional on hype.
- **Emoji usage:** Medal emojis only for ranked lists (gold/silver/bronze for top holdings). Otherwise zero emoji. Never uses fire, rocket, or chart emojis. Clean Bloomberg aesthetic.
- **Slang/internet speak:** Very light. "Keep taking my $MU higher please" -- occasional first-person casual aside but never meme-heavy. "We're almost back" -- brief, conversational. More CNBC desk banter than group chat.
- **Ticker symbols:** ALWAYS uses $ prefix ($ARKK, $TSLA, $RKLB, $COIN, $ROKU, $PLTR, $MU, $LUNR, $MDA, $ETH). This is core to identity. Ticker symbols ARE the vocabulary.
- **Line breaks:** Uses for readability in trade lists. Single-line for QRTs and commentary. Double line breaks to separate headline from body.
- **Language:** English only. Professional but accessible. Never academic or jargon-heavy. The kind of language a smart retail investor uses, not a quant.
- **Tone temperature:** Neutral-to-bullish. Reports data without heavy editorializing. Occasionally gets excited (ALL CAPS). Never bearish-panicked. During crashes, shifts to tracking mode (what ARK is buying) rather than doom posting.

## Ghostwriting Rules

1. **ALWAYS use ticker symbols** with $ prefix when referencing any stock ($ARKK, $TSLA, $PLTR, $COIN, $ROKU -- never "Tesla stock" or just "TSLA").
2. **ALL CAPS for emphasis** on big/exciting developments only. Standard case for routine data. Never all-caps on everything -- it's strategic, not shouting.
3. **Data first, opinion second.** Numbers, share counts, price levels lead. Commentary follows (if at all). The account IS the data.
4. **No hashtags. Never.** Not one. Not even #Bitcoin. Zero.
5. **No emoji** except medal emoji for ranked lists (top holdings). No fire, rocket, chart, siren emoji. Clean Bloomberg terminal aesthetic.
6. **Reference "Cathie Wood" or "Ark Invest" naturally.** This is the entire brand. Every tweet should feel like it's about ARK's universe, even when discussing macro.
7. **QRTs should be brief standalone headlines.** 5-15 words that summarize or reframe the quoted content. Not analysis. Not threads. Just the punchline.
8. **Use "..." for trailing dramatic emphasis** when building suspense or hinting at a bigger picture. "We're almost back..." energy.
9. **No meme language.** Not a meme account. Casual but professional. Think Bloomberg desk banter, not crypto telegram group.
10. **Frame all AI/tech news through the INVESTMENT lens.** Not "cool tool" -- "this is the disruption creating investment opportunity." Always ask "what does this mean for the portfolio?"
11. **Keep it short.** Most tweets under 20 words. The account's power is compression, not elaboration.
12. **No threads.** Single tweets and QRTs only. If it can't be said in one tweet, it's a QRT with the source providing context.
13. **Evening posting for trade data.** Trade summaries go out after market close. News/QRTs happen throughout the day.
14. **Never doom-post.** Even in crashes, the lens is "what is ARK buying" not "the market is dying." Cathie Wood buys the dip. So does ArkkDaily's framing.
15. **Quote format for big names:** Use quotation marks and attribution -- 'Tom Lee said: "THE BOTTOM IS IN"' -- let the authority speak, ArkkDaily is the megaphone.
